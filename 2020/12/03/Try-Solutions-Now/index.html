Try Solutions Now

Want to promote your privacy protection immediately? See some useful tools and suggestions below.

DuckDuckGo

A fully anonymous search engine with the slogan “We Don’t Track You”. You are allowed to use http “POST” request, and achieve more secure connection. The search engine also provides a “bang search” feature, which supports you to use other search engine while reducing the php parameters following the website address and sending less information to them.

Type ddg.gg to have a try!

Firefox Monitor

This is a project led by Mozilla, collecting global database leakage issues and offering checking services to users. Type your email address in Firefox Monitor to see if you have been involved in, and change a password now!

Leading Operating systems on smartphones

In MIUI, iOS, and Smartisan OS, there have been indicator features that, when apps are attempting to reach highly sensitive data, like recording voice or collecting location in the background, the operating system will raise a global notification, reminding you of potential privacy invasion.

For MIUI and ColorOS, there is a feature called “blank message”, which provides tracking apps with a blank or modified device status, instead of the unique ID of your smartphone. This makes trackers more difficult to retarget you across the Internet.

Use your own VPN for encrypted connection

In addition to fight against data mining for over personalization, we should also pay attention to basic data security. An VPN can help to provide encrypted connection, and disguise the IP address assigned to your devices. The latter feature can help to avoid online fingerprinting, too.

Stay away from untrusted surveys or psychology quizzes

“What kind of pizza are you? What does the kind of pet you have say about your personality? Which U.S. city is your perfect fit?” (Knorr)

According to Caroline Knorr in her Why You Probably Shouldn’t Take That Facebook Quiz, it is said that “ All those clicks and taps give the company information on you. While not all online tests misuse your data, you usually have to go into the quiz’s terms of service to find out which information they collect and how they use it.”

Just imagine that, if different quizzes about all aspects of your life were collected and comprehensively analyzed, what conclusion can the company get? Maybe a Google AD can help you think. See Parisian Love - YouTube, which is also one of our EAP materials.
