Why we must care?

According to Turow in an IRL podcast, “all benign data can be sensitive” (2018). The consumption habits that shopping platforms collected, the real-time location and trace of yours, the model of your devices, your search histories… All these things seem to do no harm to you, but price discrimination (a strategy used by shopping platforms that offers different prices to distinct users based on their habit) and phone call disturbance have already be results that annoy us. What if all these “benign data” are collected together in one giant company or one person? They may know, you live in area X, and at 8:00AM all family members are not at home. At that time your velocity is slow, which means your car is in the garage… The hiding meanings of data can be useful for profits, and they are also useful for crime. If you like, watch an lecture by Adam Beberg Censorship, Privacy, Anonymity, and Net Neutrality - YouTube, and you can get a deeper understanding.

There was a heated trend of criticizing Robin Li, the CEO of Baidu, one of the technology giants in China, and also around the world. Because he said “Chinese people are more open, or less sensitive to trade their privacy for convenience and efficiency”. You can see the video clip of lecture here. Users were represented, and forced to “be open”. Companies are still confidently justifying their data collection. If we do not protest, or even at least protect ourselves, the issue can only be more severe.

Fortunately, there are ongoing surveys, researches, tools and actions, which proves how important personal privacy is. More and more people are paying attention to the unequal and unsecure data collection and privacy invasion. We should hold a more serious attitude to all personal data belonging to us, and take possible actions to reduce the risks.

Be more aware, Your data weigh more, Take instant actions, and Every click matters. This is BytePrivacy, we must care.
